What Is a Career in Tech (And How Do You Get One)?

Lesson 1 - Fall Semester

← Back to All Lessons Next Lesson →

Lesson Overview

This foundational lesson introduces students to the diverse landscape of technology careers and provides a roadmap for achieving their professional goals. We'll explore various career paths, understand different types of work experiences, and learn how to strategically plan your journey into tech.

Career Foundations Professional Development

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the major career paths in CS/IT including Software Engineering, Product Management, IT, Data Science, and Cybersecurity
  • Learn the differences between internships, co-ops, and research opportunities
  • Master the technique of reverse-engineering career goals
  • Analyze real examples of student-to-intern success stories

Topics Covered

Career Paths in Technology

  • Software Engineering (SWE): Building applications, systems, and platforms
  • Product Management (PM): Bridging technical and business requirements
  • Information Technology (IT): Managing systems, infrastructure, and support
  • Data Science & Analytics: Extracting insights from data
  • Cybersecurity: Protecting systems and data
  • UX/UI Design: Creating user-centered digital experiences
  • DevOps/Cloud Engineering: Managing deployment and infrastructure

Types of Work Experiences

  • Internships: Short-term (summer) positions focused on learning
  • Co-ops: Longer-term (semester/year) work-study programs
  • Research: Academic or industry research projects
  • Part-time roles: Flexible work during school
  • Freelancing: Project-based independent work

Reverse-Engineering Your Career Goals

  1. Identify your target role and company
  2. Research required skills and qualifications
  3. Find people in those roles on LinkedIn
  4. Analyze their career paths and backgrounds
  5. Create a plan to build similar experiences
